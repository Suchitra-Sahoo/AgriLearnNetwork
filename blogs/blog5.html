Advances in Agricultural Technology

Agricultural technology has transformed farming and includes devices, machines, sensors, and information technology. Modern agriculture uses technologies such as:

Farm automation

Includes drones, autonomous tractors, robotic harvesters, automatic watering, and seeding robots

Precision agriculture

Allows farmers to control variables like moisture levels, soil conditions, and microclimates to maximize yields

Automated irrigation systems

Improves water distribution efficiency and the quality and quantity of agricultural production

Genetically modified crops

Plants altered to contain traits that benefit farmers and consumers, such as increased resistance to pests and diseases, tolerance to herbicides, better nutritional value, and resilience to adverse weather conditions

Agricultural drone technology

Scans a field from above and reports on problems like pests, infections, and a lack of essential nutrients

Ultrasound

A useful tool to judge the quality of meat

Moisture sensors

Planted underground to provide information regarding the moisture levels present at certain depths in the soil

Other examples of advanced agriculture technology include:

  • Autonomous machines
  • Sensor-equipped robots
  • Augmented reality
  • The Internet of Things (IoT)
  • EOSDA Crop Monitoring
  • Geographic Information Systems (GIS)
These technologies can help businesses be more profitable, efficient, safer, and environmentally friendly.
